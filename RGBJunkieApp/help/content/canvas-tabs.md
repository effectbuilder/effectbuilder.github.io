## One Scene, many canvases

The strip **above the workspace canvas** is your tab bar. Each tab is its own layout workspace:

* **PC case** — fans and strips inside the glass
* **Desk** — peripherals and monitor bias
* **Keyboard** — its own zone

Every **component** lives on exactly one tab. Effect slider values are remembered **per tab**, so switching tabs won’t stomp on another tab’s tuning.

## Add, rename, switch

* **+** — new tab
* Click a tab name to switch
* Rename from the tab’s context menu

The **Scene** bar sits right above this strip. Saving a Scene grabs **all** tabs and everything on them. See [Scene profiles](scene-profiles).

## Eye toggle — peek at other tabs

Next to **+**, the **eye** toggle controls components from **other** tabs:

* **Show dimmed** — see them faded on the current canvas (great for lining things up)
* **Hide** — only the active tab’s components show

RGBJunkie remembers your choice next time you open the app.

## Keyboard shortcuts

| Key | Action |
| --- | ------ |
| **1**–**9** | Move the current selection to tab 1–9 (first tab is **1**). Numpad works too. |
| **Ctrl+A** | Select everything visible on the **active** tab (respects the eye toggle) |

You can also move a component from the device tree or its context menu.
