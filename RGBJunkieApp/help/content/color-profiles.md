## Gradients you can reuse everywhere

Open **Settings → Colors → Color profiles**.

A **color profile** is a named gradient — multiple color stops you can plug into effects and layers instead of picking one random hue at a time.

## Build one by hand

1. **Add profile** or pick an existing row.
2. Drag **stops** on the gradient bar, or type hex values if you’re picky.
3. Give it a name you’ll recognize in effect dropdowns.

## Or let RGBJunkie guess from a vibe

Type a short mood — “midnight rain”, “sunset beach”, “lawyer office” (we’ve seen worse) — and click **Generate profile**. RGBJunkie builds a multi-stop gradient from online color lookup when it can, or a built-in palette when you’re offline.

Each profile can keep a **phrase** field so you can regenerate later if you want a fresh take.

## Use profiles in effects

Lots of effects (Fan Tracer layers, and plenty of others) have a **color profile** dropdown. Pick your gradient instead of a single color or a rainbow sweep.

Still seeing a rainbow after you picked a profile? Double-check it’s selected on the **right layer** in that effect’s settings — easy to miss.

## Profiles vs calibration

**Calibration** (**Settings → Colors → Calibration**) fixes hardware that lies about red and green. **Profiles** are the fun creative part — they don’t fix a tinty strip on their own. See [Troubleshooting — wrong colors](troubleshooting-wrong-colors).
